{% extends 'special-sessions/_special-session.html' %} {% set code='machs' %} {% block abstract %} There has been a growing interest in hybrid systems in recent years. Such systems exhibit both continuous (e.g. differential equations) and discrete (e.g. difference equations) dynamical behavior and have important applications in various industrial and technological areas such as communication, complex networks, biotechnology, artificial intelligence, switching circuits in power electronics, spacecrafts control, and ecosystems management. This special session intends to provide an opportunity to learn and share new developments and to present research results in modeling, analysis and control in hybrid systems. {% endblock %}
