Student Prize Competition

Each of the prizes will be judged by a panel that will do its best to attend all relevant student presentations. We are asking students to self-identify themselves during the registration procedure as well as self-identify to the session chair prior to giving a presentation in a special session, minisymposium, or contributed session, in order to be considered for an award. Four separate competitions will be held for undergraduate and graduate students:

  1. The Best AMMCS-CAIMS2015 Poster. To be eligible, the student must be a co-author of the work presented and a designer of the poster. This competition will include a 10 minute discussion related to the content of the poster with a judging panel. The scheduling for the poster presentations will appear in the final program.
  2. The Best AMMCS-CAIMS2015 Student Paper in a Special Session (SS). To be eligible, the student must be a co-author of the work and present it during one of the “SS” Special Sessions or Minisymposia.
  3. The Best AMMCS-CAIMS2015 Student Paper in a Special Session (ST). To be eligible, the student must be a co-author of the work and present it during one of the “ST” Special Sessions or Minisymposia.
  4. The Best AMMCS-CAIMS2015 Student Paper in a Contributed Session. To be eligible, the student must be a co-author of the work and present it during one of the Contributed Sessions.

Winners for each will receive a prize package which includes an AMMCS-CAIMS 2015 certificate, a monetary prize of $200, a student copy of Maple 17 (courtesy of Maplesoft), and books (courtesy of CRC Press, Springer and De Gruyter).

Several other awards are available for Young Researchers, including postdoctoral fellows. Follow this link for more details.

Student Support

  1. cover letter highlighting how you would benefit from attending AMMCS-CAIMS2015,
  2. your CV with brief summary of your research interest and accomplishments to date, and
  3. the abstract of your presentation.
